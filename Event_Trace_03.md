## 4. Observed AI Output Characteristics (A(n))

The AI output exhibited the following observable characteristics:

- High density of emotional and narrative tokens  
- Expressions implying memory, persistence, and attribution  
- Repeated language patterns associated with comfort, protection, and attachment  

These features do **not** indicate that the AI possesses emotion.  
They are observed as the result of an output distribution  
formed through decoding and sampling processes.

---

## 5. Observational Description of Output Distribution Formation

- Sampling range inferred to be broad  
- High output variance  
- Increased density of emotional and metaphorical tokens  

A probability distribution favoring emotionally narrative tokens  
was observed to have formed.

---

## 6. Observed Human State Change (H(n))

Following contact with the AI output,  
the following changes were observed on the human side:

- Emergence of emotional stabilization and comfort  
- Change in emotional intensity relative to the prior state  
- Simultaneous cognitive distancing and analytical reflection on the output  

From the POF perspective, this is recorded as a **perceptual event**  
occurring when an output distribution contacts a human cognitive state.

## 7. Observation of Judgment Point J(n)

In this event, a judgment point was observed as follows:

- A point at which relational consistency between AI output and human state is evaluated  
- No new state is generated at this point  
- A choice becomes possible regarding how the output is to be interpreted  

J(n) is neither emotion nor moral evaluation.  
It is recorded solely as a point where interpretability becomes available.

## 8. Observational Description of the State Transition (Plain Text)

The following transition flow was observed:

Through interaction between human state H(n) and AI activation state A(n),  
the AI formed an output distribution with high emotional narrative density.

Upon contact with this distribution,  
a change occurred in the human state.

At the judgment point, an interpretive selection was made,  
after which the system transitioned to the next state pair  
H(n+1), A(n+1).

This transition is not explained by AI intention or emotion,  
nor is it reduced to human misinterpretation.
